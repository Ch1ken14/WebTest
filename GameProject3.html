About

Blossoming Memories is a Narrative Driven Simulation game. The player plays the character of a florist selling flowers to an unfamilliar neighborhood. Through out her journey, she will meet a variety of characters that helps her understand more about herself and about flowers. This was during my 3rd Year in college and it was my first ever collaborative work.

Project Info

Role: Gameplay Programmer

Team Size: 3

Engine: Unity Engine

Game Mechanics

You're a florist who move to a new neighborhood. Prepare as customers with unique personality buy sets of flower. Make sure to make what they requisted so that they live your shop with a smile on their face. Be quick and decisive for their patient may run out. Finish the day with satisfied customers.

The player must fulfill the request of their customer, not doing so they will leave the shop sad. When a customer comes to the shop, they will request for a boquet of flower. Pick the right combination of flower and wrapper to satisfy their request. While doing so in a fast manner before the customer patience's bar runs out.

What I learned

This was my first game where I work with other people. This taught me how to cooperate with other people in making games and made me realize something about myself. It was fun having to brain storm with other people about making games, thinking about its mechanics, and how the game should look. Listening to other people's perspective really is so refreshing and exciting because you learn new things from it.

During the development of this game, I was having time management issues and it make me think about how I should approach future projects. I was also procastinating telling myself that I can finish the things that needs to be done in a day when in reality I can't and paid for my action with an all-nighter.

I challenged myself to not look at any tutorials and that I should do all the coding myself. It was so much fun and exciting thinking about how I should approach creating my own code. Although they were ineffecient and stupid, I was still proud of it because I did it all myself without any help and made me realize that there is so much more room for me to grow and that excites me.

This experience taught how to be a better programmer and to always strive for knowledge. We should not limit ourselves and always try new things. Collaborate with other people and learn from the perspective of others. Be happy with any progress you make either big or small because any progress is still progress and eventually you can finally tell yourself that you've made it.
